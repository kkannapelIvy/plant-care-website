Flower Care

There are many different types of flowers in the world. Here are some care tips for the more common types of flowers.

Rose

  • Roses seem like a hard flower to care for, but that is far from the truth. Roses grow best if they are planted at the start of spring in a location that will give them atleast 6 hours of sunlight daily. Roses require watering when the top of the soil is dry, and avoid getting the leaves wet while watering.

Sunflower

  • The best time to plant sunflowers is right after the spring frost has passed. Usually this is around the month of April. Sunflowers require nutrient rich soil and around 7 hours of sunlight per day. Depending on the type of sunflower, a support such as a fence might be needed for the flower while it is growing.

Daisy

  • Daisies should be planted either in spring or early fall to provide optimal time to grow. It should be planeted in soil that can drain water well, and be in a location where it can get a good amount of sunlight.

Orchids

  • Orchids like to be planted in areas that are warmer. Usually around 70 degrees Fahrenheit . Orchids should be watered every 7 - 10 days. Depending on the type of Orchids, supports might be needed for the plant to support their weight once grown.

Tulips

  • Tulips grown in the spring in locations with a lot of sun and soil that can drain well. Water the tulips a lot right after planeting, but after that they don't need a lot of water, except if there is a long time without rain.
